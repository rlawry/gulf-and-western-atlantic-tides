Equilibrium tide refers to the idealized tidal response from the Moon and Sun on a perfectly uniform ocean surface.

MLLW means Mean Lower Low Water: the long-term average height of the lowest tide recorded each day. Tide values here are relative to that baseline.

The animation shows tide-gauge locations horizontally by longitude. Each dot moves vertically according to predicted tide height at that site.

The Moon’s marker is plotted using its sublunar longitude, which is the longitude directly beneath the Moon as Earth rotates.

If the Moon’s longitude lies outside the visible gage range, it is hidden until it reappears within the current span.
